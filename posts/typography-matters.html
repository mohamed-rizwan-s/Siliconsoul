Typography Matters - A Guide to Beautiful Web Text

Master the art of web typography to create readable, beautiful, and accessible text content.

typographydesigncss

Typography is the voice of your content. Good typography goes unnoticed—it simply makes reading effortless. Bad typography, on the other hand, creates friction and fatigue.

The Fundamentals

Font Selection

Choose fonts that match your content's personality:

  • Serif fonts (Georgia, Times): Traditional, authoritative, great for long-form reading

  • Sans-serif fonts (Inter, Helvetica): Modern, clean, versatile
  • Monospace fonts (JetBrains Mono, Fira Code): Technical, code-focused

For this blog, we use Inter—a modern, highly-legible sans-serif designed specifically for screens.

Line Height

Line height (leading) affects readability:

  • Headings: 1.2–1.3 (tight)

  • Body text: 1.5–1.7 (comfortable)
  • Small text: 1.4–1.5 (slightly tighter)

CODEBLOCK1

Spacing and Measure

Letter Spacing

  • Body text: Default (0)

  • Headings: Slight negative tracking (-0.02em)
  • All-caps text: Positive tracking (0.05em)

CODEBLOCK2

Measure (Line Length)

Optimal line length is 45–75 characters. Too long and readers lose their place; too short and reading feels choppy.

Accessibility Considerations

  • Minimum font size: 16px for body text
  • Contrast ratio: At least 4.5:1 for normal text
  • Line height: At least 1.5 for body text
  • Allow zooming: Never disable user zoom

    Conclusion

    Great typography is invisible—it lets your content shine. By mastering these fundamentals, you create experiences that are not just beautiful, but genuinely easier to read.

    Remember: typography is about serving the reader. Every decision should make the text more accessible, more readable, and more enjoyable.
